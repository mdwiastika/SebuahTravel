Cultural Destination

Explore the Cultural Heritage of East Java

Embrace the rich culture of East Java by visiting historical sites, ancient temples and traditional villages. Discover the fascinating beauty of art and architecture and experience the warm hospitality of the local people.

Most Popular Destination

Jetis Batik Village

Jetis Batik Village, Sidoarjo, was founded in 1675 and is the center of the batik industry. Known for its distinctive motifs such as peacocks and sugar cane flowers, it reflects the cultural values and natural surroundings. Positive impacts include increasing the welfare of craftsmen and awareness of local culture.

Kemiren Village

Kemiren Osing Tourism Village offers unique Osing culture and interesting activities such as traditional gedhogan and coffee-making traditions, as well as Osing Barong cultural performances and traditional Osing houses to explore.

Ambarawa Railway Museum

The Bondowoso Railway Museum, located at Bondowoso Station, is the first railway museum in East Java and the fourth in Indonesia. Founded in 1893 by Staatssporwegen (SS), this museum stores the history of the Bondowoso railway.

Best Activities For You to Experience

Experience the rich history and traditions through the exploration of fascinating cultural heritage and enchanting art events.

Visit Historical Sites
Visiting historical sites provides insight into the history and culture of the area. Visitors can explore historical remains, such as temples or traditional villages, and learn about the rich cultural heritage.
Seeing an Art Performance
Watching a local art show is a great way to understand the culture and traditions of the area. Visitors can enjoy dance, music or theater depicting local stories and important cultural values.
Learn Local Cuisine
Trying local culinary delights is an experience that should not be missed in cultural tourism destinations. Visitors can learn about traditional ingredients and recipes, as well as sample regional specialties.
Cultural Festival
Attending cultural festivals is a fun way to celebrate and appreciate cultural diversity. These festivals usually feature art performances, craft exhibitions, and other cultural activities that enrich the tourist experience.

Jetis Batik VIllage

Jetis Batik Village, Sidoarjo, has been a center for the development of the batik industry since 1675 when Mbah Mulyadi brought the batik tradition to the area. From Chinese entrepreneurs in the 20th century to becoming an official tourism sector in 2008, this industry has experienced rapid development. Jetis Batik is known for its distinctive motifs such as peacocks and sugar cane flowers, reflecting the cultural values and natural surroundings. Positive impacts include increasing the welfare of craftsmen and awareness of local culture.

Kemiren Village

Osing Kemiren Traditional Tourism Village, as a tourism destination in Banyuwangi, combines cultural and natural diversity with friendly homestay accommodation, strict health protocol standards, and the integration of digital technology, while enriching the tourist experience through cultural, culinary attractions and annual events, and encouraging development of a community-based creative economy with superior products such as batik, pecel pitik, and Jaran Goyang kemiren coffee, received recognition as a Sustainable Tourism Village..

Ambarawa Railway Museum

The Bondowoso Railway Museum, located at Bondowoso (Bo) Station, is the first railway museum in East Java and the fourth in Indonesia. As part of Bondowoso Station which was built in 1893 by Staatssporwegen (SS), this museum stores the history of the Bondowoso railway and is an important witness to the Death Carriage Tragedy that occurred at this place, where 46 Indonesian fighters died in resistance to Dutch Military Aggression.

Singosari Temple

Singosari Temple, the last legacy of the 14th century Singosari Kingdom, was founded as a tribute to King Kertanegara. Even though it was not actually completed, this temple is a marker of the glory of the kingdom. Discovered by Nicholaus Engelhard, a Dutchman, in 1803, this temple has several nicknames and the largest guardian statue in the world, Dwarapala. The location is in Candirenggo Village, Malang Regency, East Java, with free access for visitors every day from 07.00 to 16.00.

Brahu Temple

Brahu Temple, in Mojokerto, East Java, is a historical site from the 15th century AD which is thought to be the place where the bodies of the Brawijaya kings were burned. Made of red brick, this temple has beautiful geometric patterns. Although without reliefs, ancient artifacts show links to Buddhism. The restoration was completed in 1995. In the surrounding area, there are other temples such as Muteran Temple and Tengah Temple, adding to the historical tourist attraction in East Java.

Penataran Temple

Panataran Temple, the largest Hindu (Siwaitis) site in East Java, is located in Penataran Village, Nglegok, about 12 km north of Blitar. Built by the Kediri Kingdom and used until the Majapahit period, the reliefs depict analogous stories of the lives of figures such as Ramayana and Krisnayana, similar to the stories of Ken Arok and Ken Dedes. Panataran Temple tourism helps stimulate the local economy with stalls selling souvenirs and additional facilities such as baths or swimming pools built by the Blitar Regency Government to attract more tourists.

Kedaton Temple

Kedaton Temple, located in Lawang Kedaton hamlet, Andung Biru village, Tris sub-district, is a 6 square meter Hindu temple with the year written on the steps. Built around the end of the XIV century, this temple is made of Andesite stone and is surrounded by fences and wire to protect it. Formerly a place of worship for Hindu Dekte Shiva, Kedaton Temple has a mysterious relief about the story of Arjunawiwaha on the west side which tells the story of Arjuna in search of a powerful weapon.

Trinil Museum

The Trinil Museum not only stores a collection of ancient human and animal fossils, but also provides various recreational facilities such as an outbound arena, campground and play area for children. With its large area, this museum is often a tourist destination for families and school groups who want to learn while playing and enjoying the beauty of unspoiled nature.

Bangkalan Cakraningrat

The Bangkalan Cakraningrat Museum, founded on March 13 2008 by the Governor of East Java, Imam Utomo, is part of the Bangkalan Regency Youth, Sports, Culture and Tourism Office. Originally founded in the 1950s to preserve Bangkalan's historical objects, the museum was moved to a new building in 2008. The collection includes Dutch cannons, traditional weapons, lontar manuscripts, gamelan and various other ancient artifacts.

Brawijaya Museum

The Brawijaya Museum in Malang, East Java, is the right destination to commemorate the services of heroes, especially in commemoration of Heroes' Day. Founded in 1962 by Brigadier General TNI (Ret.) Soerachman, this museum has become a symbol of national struggle and strength. With a collection that includes vehicles from the past, hero clothing, war weapons, and even the Carriage of Death which holds a tragic story, the Brawijaya Museum plays an important role as a center for education, recreation and scientific research.
